Getting There

  • By Air: The quickest way to reach Cox's Bazar is by flying from Dhaka, the capital city. Domestic airlines operate several flights daily, making it a convenient option for travelers.
  • By Road: Buses and private cars are also viable options. The journey from Dhaka to Cox's Bazar by road takes about 10-12 hours, offering scenic views of the countryside.
  • By Train: Although there's no direct train to Cox's Bazar, you can take a train to Chittagong and then travel by bus or car to Cox's Bazar, which is about 150 kilometers away.

Accommodation

Cox's Bazar offers a wide range of accommodations, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly hotels. Some popular options include:
  1. Luxury Resorts: Sayeman Beach Resort, Long Beach Hotel, and Ocean Paradise Hotel & Resort.
  2. Mid-Range Hotels: Hotel Sea Crown, Hotel The Cox Today, and Praasad Paradise Hotel & Resort.
  3. Budget Options: Sea View Hotel, Hotel Kollol by J&Z Group, and Hotel Coral Reef.

Attractions

  1. Cox's Bazar Beach: The main attraction, known for its gentle waves and golden sands, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beach sports.
  2. Himchari National Park: Located about 12 kilometers south of Cox's Bazar, this park features lush greenery, waterfalls, and panoramic views of the Bay of Bengal.
  3. Inani Beach: About 35 kilometers from Cox's Bazar, Inani Beach is less crowded and offers stunning coral rock formations and crystal-clear waters.
  4. Saint Martin's Island: A small coral island in the northeastern part of the Bay of Bengal, known for its clear blue waters, vibrant marine life, and serene environment. Accessible by boat from Teknaf, which is about 85 kilometers from Cox's Bazar.
  5. Radiant Fish World: An aquarium showcasing various marine species, providing an educational and entertaining experience for visitors.

Activities

  • Beach Activities: Swimming, surfing, and beach volleyball are popular activities. You can also enjoy horse riding along the beach.
  • Boat Rides: Take a boat trip to explore nearby islands and enjoy the scenic beauty of the coastline
  • Local Markets: Visit local markets such as Burmese Market to buy handicrafts, souvenirs, and traditional Burmese products.
  • Cultural Experiences: Engage with local culture by visiting traditional villages, attending cultural shows, and tasting local cuisine.
